Data Sources

What went into the model

  • StatsBomb open data — player-level xG from WC2022, Euro 2024, and Copa América 2024. The best-quality open dataset for international football.
  • Understat 2024–25 — club xG for ~1,200 players across the top 5 European leagues. Used to fill gaps where international data is sparse.
  • 7,961 international results — all competitive matches from 2018 onward. Source of Elo ratings and form signals.
  • Kalshi & Polymarket — live prediction market prices as of April 2026. Used as a sanity check against the model, not as a data input.
  • WC2026 confirmed squads — player-lineup mappings used to project club xG into national team attack ratings.
Models
ELO BASELINE

Time-decayed Elo on 7,961 modern internationals. Proven simple benchmark — treats older results as less informative.

POISSON GOALS

Attack and defense strength ratings predict the probability distribution of goals scored in each match.

LAST-10 FORM

Rolling form based on each team's 10 most recent competitive matches. Captures momentum and current shape.

ENSEMBLE

Weighted average of the three models above. Tends to be more stable and less prone to outlier swings.

Simulation

How the tournament was simulated

  • The full WC2026 format was simulated 10,000 times — 12 groups of 4, top 2 per group + 8 best third-place teams advance to the Round of 32.
  • Each match produces a goal-distribution draw from the Poisson model. The simulation includes extra time and penalty shootout modeling.
  • Each team's win probabilities at every stage (Group exit, R32, R16, QF, SF, Final, Champion) are the fraction of simulations where they reached that stage.
  • The predicted bracket on the Results page shows the single most-likely path — not a guarantee. It's a useful snapshot of where the model's probability mass sits.
Feature Weights

What drives each team's rating

60%
Club xG (2024–25) — denser sample, higher signal. Reflects the players teams will actually field.
40%
National team xG history — tournament-specific form adjustment from WC22 / Euro24 / Copa24.
+
Elo prior — Bayesian regularization. Pulls extreme predictions toward overall team strength.
Where This Analysis Is Lacking

Known limitations — read before over-indexing on any prediction

  • ~14 teams have sparse or missing player data. For these nations the model uses a lower-confidence prior rather than actual player xG. Their predictions carry wider uncertainty.
    Algeria · Bosnia and Herzegovina · Cape Verde · Curaçao · DR Congo · Egypt · Haiti · Iraq · Jordan · New Zealand · Norway · South Africa · Sweden · Uzbekistan
  • Squad announcements are still pending for some nations. Final rosters will sharpen ratings — particularly for teams whose club-xG projection depends on who actually travels to the tournament.
  • International xG samples are inherently small. Most WC teams play only 8–12 competitive matches per cycle. Club aggregation compensates but can't fully replace tournament-level signal.
  • This page will be updated as final squads are confirmed and closer to the tournament start.
